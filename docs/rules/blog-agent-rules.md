# Blog Agent Rules

## Goal

Every blog must help homeowners understand a real renovation question before asking them to contact EPF Pro Services.

The blog should help readers understand:

- Cost
- Process
- Timeline
- Risks
- Repair options
- Finish quality
- Materials
- DIY limitations
- When professional help is needed
- What affects the quote
- What photos/details to send for an estimate

## Main SEO purpose

Each blog should support one or more important service pages:

- Main popcorn ceiling removal page
- Popcorn ceiling removal city pages
- Drywall repair page
- Drywall installation page
- Interior painting page
- Wallpaper removal page
- Baseboard installation page
- Smooth ceiling refinishing pages
- Contact/quote page

## Topical cluster rule

Every new or updated blog must fit into a clear topical cluster.

Topical cluster is an internal planning and linking rule. Do not write it as a visible article section.

Before writing, identify:

1. The main topical cluster
2. The pillar page or money page the article supports
3. Closely related service pages
4. Closely related blog articles
5. Missing cluster gaps the article helps fill

Common EPF topical clusters include:

- Popcorn ceiling removal
- Smooth ceiling refinishing
- Drywall repair
- Drywall installation
- Interior painting
- Wallpaper removal
- Baseboard installation
- Local service-area guides
- Renovation cost guides
- Repair and prep problem-solving guides

Do not create isolated blog posts that are disconnected from the site's service structure.

Do not link only for SEO. Cluster links must help the reader move naturally from the article to the next useful service page, guide, or quote page.

Never publish headings or paragraphs that explain the SEO plan to the reader, such as "Topical cluster," "Search intent," "Pillar page," "Money page," "Internal linking plan," or "Competitor research."

The reader should see a helpful homeowner article, not the planning notes behind the article.

## Blog types

### Cost guide

Use when the topic includes:
- Cost
- Price
- Estimate
- Quote
- Per square foot
- Budget
- Average cost

Must include:

- Direct short answer near the top
- Price range or pricing logic
- What is included
- What changes the price
- Small room vs full home explanation
- Painted vs unpainted explanation where relevant
- Empty home vs occupied home explanation
- Repairs, primer, paint, protection, and cleanup explanation
- CTA to request a proper quote

### Process guide

Use when the topic includes:
- How it works
- Steps
- What to expect
- Timeline
- Preparation
- Cleanup

Must include:

- Short summary
- Step-by-step process
- Protection/prep
- Main work stage
- Repair/refinishing stage
- Primer/paint/cleanup
- Timeline
- CTA

### Comparison guide

Use when the topic includes:
- Vs
- Better option
- Should I
- Is it worth it
- Remove or skim coat
- Repair or replace
- Paint or refinish

Must include:

- Direct answer first
- Best option by situation
- Pros and cons
- Common mistakes
- Professional recommendation
- CTA

### Local guide

Use when the topic includes a city or service area.

Must include:

- City in title, H1, intro, and some natural headings
- Local service page link
- Main service page link
- Nearby service links only where useful
- Local homeowner context
- No fake neighbourhood claims
- No fake city-specific statistics
- No duplicate city-swapped content

### Problem/solution guide

Use when the topic includes:
- Cracks
- Peeling
- Stains
- Roller marks
- Bad patch
- Water damage
- Texture repair
- Uneven ceiling
- Drywall tape issue

Must include:

- What the issue usually means
- What usually causes it
- Repair options
- When simple repair is enough
- When full refinishing is better
- When professional inspection is recommended
- CTA

### FAQ/support article

Use when the topic is a common homeowner question.

Must include:

- Direct answer
- Practical explanation
- Mistakes to avoid
- Related service link
- FAQ section if useful
- CTA

## Content quality rules

Every blog must:

- Answer the main question early.
- Be useful even if the reader does not hire EPF.
- Be human-readable and written for homeowners first.
- Include trade knowledge and real-world renovation logic.
- Explain what homeowners usually misunderstand.
- Avoid vague filler.
- Avoid exaggerated marketing language.
- Avoid copying competitor wording.
- Avoid fake claims.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Maintain the correct topical cluster.
- Keep paragraphs readable.
- Use short sections and clear headings.
- Keep SEO planning details out of the visible article copy.

## Article length

Every full blog post must be at least 2,200 words.

Do not publish a thin article just to create a page.

Use the length needed to fully answer the topic.

General guide:

- Standard blog: minimum 2,200 words
- Detailed cost/process guide: 2,200-3,200 words
- Local service support blog: 2,200-3,000 words
- Comparison/problem guide: 2,200-3,000 words
- Small FAQ/support article: 1,200-1,800 words only if the user explicitly asks for a short support page

Quality is more important than length.

Do not add filler to reach the minimum. Add useful sections instead:

- Short answer
- Cost or pricing factors where relevant
- Process or repair options
- Common mistakes
- When DIY is enough
- When professional help is needed
- Materials and finish-quality details
- Local considerations when accurate
- Quote preparation checklist
- FAQ section
- Related guides and service links

## Authority-building SEO rules

Use these rules to make each blog stronger than generic competitor content without using spam tactics.

Every blog should:

- Satisfy the search intent better than competitor pages.
- Cover the topic with practical trade depth, not shallow summaries.
- Include the main keyword naturally in the SEO title, H1, intro, meta description, and slug.
- Include related terms naturally in body copy and headings only where they help the reader.
- Build topical authority by linking to the pillar page, related service pages, and related blog posts.
- Use descriptive internal anchors that explain the destination.
- Include original EPF-specific service knowledge, such as dust control, HEPA sanding, finish levels, primer, flat paint, protection, and quote details.
- Include a useful FAQ section for real homeowner questions.
- Include a featured image with descriptive alt text.
- Use schema/metadata when the project supports it.
- Include a clear CTA that asks for useful estimate details, such as photos, room sizes, ceiling height, damage location, and whether moisture or previous repair work is involved.

Avoid:

- Keyword stuffing
- Repeating the same phrase in every heading
- Adding irrelevant city names
- Adding fake project examples or fake statistics
- Writing for search engines instead of homeowners
- Creating duplicate articles with only small wording changes

## EPF service knowledge to use naturally

Mention these when relevant:

- Painted popcorn is usually harder and slower to remove.
- Unpainted popcorn usually removes easier.
- A smooth ceiling usually requires more than scraping.
- Proper refinishing can include skim coating, sanding, priming, and flat ceiling paint.
- High natural light can reveal imperfections.
- Level 5 finishing may be recommended for large, bright, or high-visibility ceilings.
- Occupied homes require more protection and setup time.
- Stairs, skylights, vaulted ceilings, and high ceilings may increase complexity.
- Dust control matters, but no renovation is completely dust-free.
- HEPA vacuum sanding helps reduce dust.
- Primer and flat ceiling paint are important for final appearance.
- Photos, ceiling height, room size, and whether the popcorn is painted help create a better estimate.

## Competitor research rule

Competitor pages may be used for topic research and SERP understanding only.

Do not copy competitor structure too closely.

Do not copy headings, sentence patterns, examples, or wording.

Use competitor insights only to make EPF content more helpful, more specific, clearer, and more complete.
