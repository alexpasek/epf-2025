## Types of Drywall Damage

### 1. **Small Holes** (Less than 1 inch)

**Common causes:**

- Doorknob impacts
- Nail or screw holes
- Small furniture bumps
- Picture hanging mistakes

**Repair difficulty:** Easy (DIY-friendly)  
**Time:** 30 minutes - 1 hour  
**Cost:** $5-$20 (DIY)

### 2. **Medium Holes** (1-6 inches)

**Common causes:**

- Furniture impacts
- Moving accidents
- Plumbing access holes
- Electrical work

**Repair difficulty:** Moderate (requires patches)  
**Time:** 2-4 hours  
**Cost:** $20-$100 (DIY), $150-$300 (professional)

### 3. **Large Holes** (6+ inches)

**Common causes:**

- Major impacts
- Water damage
- Structural issues
- Renovation work

**Repair difficulty:** Challenging  
**Time:** 4-8 hours + drying  
**Cost:** $50-$150 (DIY), $300-$800 (professional)

### 4. **Cracks**

**Common causes:**

- House settling
- Temperature changes
- Foundation movement
- Poor initial installation

**Types:**

- Hairline cracks (cosmetic)
- Structural cracks (foundation issues)
- Corner cracks (framing movement)

**Repair difficulty:** Easy to moderate  
**Time:** 1-3 hours  
**Cost:** $10-$50 (DIY), $100-$400 (professional)

### 5. **Water Damage**

**Common causes:**

- Roof leaks
- Plumbing failures
- Flooding
- High humidity

**Signs:**

- Brown stains
- Soft, crumbling drywall
- Mold growth
- Peeling paint or texture

**Repair difficulty:** Moderate to challenging  
**Time:** 1-3 days  
**Cost:** $100-$500+ (professional recommended)

## Small Hole Repair (DIY Method)

### Step-by-Step: Nail Holes & Dings

**Time:** 30 minutes + drying  
**Difficulty:** ⭐ Easy

#### 1. **Clean the Area**

- Remove loose debris
- Wipe with damp cloth
- Let dry completely

#### 2. **Apply Spackling**

- Scoop small amount on putty knife
- Press into hole firmly
- Scrape smooth, slightly overfilled

#### 3. **Let Dry**

- Wait 1-2 hours
- Check manufacturer instructions

#### 4. **Sand Smooth**

- Use 120-grit sandpaper
- Sand until flush with wall
- Wipe away dust

#### 5. **Prime & Paint**

- Apply primer (prevents flashing)
- Let dry (30 min - 1 hour)
- Paint to match wall

**Pro tip:** For multiple small holes, fill all at once, then sand and paint together for efficiency.

## Medium Hole Repair (1-6 inches)

### Method 1: Self-Adhesive Mesh Patch

**Time:** 2-3 hours + drying  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ Moderate

#### 1. **Prepare the Hole**

```
- Clean edges of loose debris
- Don't enlarge hole unnecessarily
- Ensure edges are firm
```

#### 2. **Apply Mesh Patch**

- Peel backing off adhesive mesh
- Center over hole
- Press firmly around edges

#### 3. **First Coat - Joint Compound**

- Apply thin layer over patch
- Extend 2-3 inches beyond patch
- Let dry 4-6 hours

#### 4. **Second Coat - Feather Edges**

- Apply second, wider coat
- Feather edges 6-8 inches
- Let dry overnight

#### 5. **Third Coat - Final Smoothing**

- Apply thin final coat
- Blend with surrounding wall
- Let dry completely

#### 6. **Sand & Finish**

- Sand with 120-grit paper
- Wipe dust with damp cloth
- Prime and paint

### Method 2: California Patch (No Backing)

**Best for:** Holes without accessible backing

**Time:** 2-4 hours + drying  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ Moderate-Advanced

#### 1. **Cut Square Patch**

- Measure hole
- Cut drywall square 2" larger
- Score back of drywall 1" from edges

#### 2. **Snap Off Extra Drywall**

- Leave paper facing intact
- Creates paper border around patch

#### 3. **Trace & Cut Wall**

- Trace patch onto wall
- Cut wall opening to match
- Remove damaged drywall

#### 4. **Install Patch**

- Apply joint compound to back of paper
- Press patch into opening
- Smooth paper edges flat

#### 5. **Mud, Tape & Finish**

- Apply joint compound
- Let dry and sand
- Repeat 2-3 times

## Large Hole Repair (6+ inches)

### Professional Method: Full Backing

**Time:** 4-8 hours + drying  
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Advanced

#### 1. **Cut Square Opening**

```
- Mark rectangle around damage
- Cut with drywall saw
- Remove damaged section
- Expose studs if possible
```

#### 2. **Install Backing**

**Option A: Studs Available**

- Screw patch directly to studs

**Option B: No Studs (Floating Patch)**

- Cut furring strips 6" longer than hole
- Slide behind drywall top and bottom
- Screw through drywall into strips

#### 3. **Cut & Install Patch**

- Measure opening precisely
- Cut new drywall to fit
- Install with 1-1/4" screws
- Screw heads slightly recessed

#### 4. **Tape Seams**

- Apply joint compound
- Embed paper drywall tape
- Smooth with putty knife
- Let dry 4-6 hours

#### 5. **Apply Multiple Coats**

**First coat:** Cover tape, fill screw holes  
**Second coat:** Wider, feather edges  
**Third coat:** Final smoothing

Each coat must dry completely (4-24 hours)

#### 6. **Sand & Finish**

- Sand with 120-grit, then 220-grit
- Prime entire repair area
- Apply texture if needed
- Paint to match

**Pro tip:** Feathering edges 12-18 inches beyond repair makes patches invisible.

## Crack Repair Guide

### Hairline Cracks

**Time:** 1 hour + drying  
**Difficulty:** ⭐ Easy

1. **Widen crack** slightly with utility knife (creates V-groove)
2. **Fill with joint compound** or paintable caulk
3. **Smooth flush** with putty knife
4. **Sand, prime, and paint**

### Recurring Cracks (Structural)

**Warning signs:**

- Crack returns after repair
- Grows wider over time
- Doors/windows stick
- Floor slopes

**Action:** These indicate foundation or framing issues. **Call a structural engineer** before cosmetic repair.

### Corner Cracks

**Common cause:** House settling or poor corner bead installation

**Repair:**

1. Remove old corner bead if damaged
2. Install new metal corner bead
3. Apply 3 coats joint compound
4. Sand smooth and paint

## Water Damage Repair

### ⚠️ Critical First Steps

**Before repairing water-damaged drywall:**

1. **Find and fix the leak source**
   - Roof inspection
   - Plumbing check
   - HVAC assessment

2. **Test for mold**
   - Visible mold = professional removal
   - Hidden mold testing available

3. **Ensure complete drying**
   - Use fans and dehumidifiers
   - May take 3-7 days
   - Use moisture meter to confirm

### When to Replace vs. Repair

**Replace if:**

- Soft or crumbling
- Visible mold growth
- Sagging or deformed
- Strong musty odor

**May repair if:**

- Only staining present
- Drywall still firm
- No mold detected
- Small affected area

### Water Stain Removal

1. **Seal with stain-blocking primer**  
   (Kilz or Zinsser BIN)

2. **Apply two coats** for dark stains

3. **Paint to match** wall color

**Important:** Never paint over water stains without primer. Stains will bleed through.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. **Skipping Primer**

Paint applied directly over repairs shows "flashing" - dull spots visible in certain light.

**Solution:** Always prime repairs before painting.

### 2. **Not Feathering Edges**

Visible lines around patch.

**Solution:** Extend each coat 2-4 inches beyond previous coat.

### 3. **Rushing Between Coats**

Wet sanding, cracking, or poor adhesion.

**Solution:** Wait full drying time between coats (4-24 hours).

### 4. **Over-Sanding**

Damages paper face, creates fuzzy texture.

**Solution:** Use light pressure, multiple passes.

### 5. **Wrong Joint Compound**

All-purpose compound shrinks in large repairs.

**Solution:** Use setting-type compound for holes 2"+.

### 6. **Ignoring Water Source**

Mold growth, recurring damage.

**Solution:** Always fix leak before repairing drywall.

### 7. **DIY Complex Repairs**

Poor finish, visible patches, wasted time.

**Solution:** Call professionals for large or multiple repairs.

## Texture Matching Guide

### Smooth Walls

Easiest to match. Multiple coats of joint compound + sanding creates invisible repair.

### Orange Peel (Most Common)

**DIY:** Use texture gun or "hopper" sprayer  
**Professional:** Matched perfectly with commercial equipment

### Knockdown Texture

**Process:**

1. Apply texture spray
2. Let dry 10-15 minutes
3. Knock down high spots with trowel

**Professional recommended** - timing is critical

### Skip Trowel

**DIY difficulty:** Very challenging  
**Professional recommended:** Yes - requires experience

**Pro tip:** Save leftover texture from original application for future repairs.

## Preventing Future Damage

### 1. **Install Door Stops**

Prevents doorknob holes - the #1 cause of drywall damage.

### 2. **Use Wall Anchors Properly**

- Drywall anchors for lightweight items
- Find studs for heavy items (TVs, shelves)
- Never overload drywall

### 3. **Address Foundation Issues**

Settling causes recurring cracks. Fix foundation = stop cracks.

### 4. **Maintain Proper Humidity**

- Keep at 30-50% RH
- Prevents cracking and mold
- Use dehumidifiers in basements

### 5. **Fix Leaks Immediately**

Water is drywall's enemy. Address leaks within 24-48 hours.

### 6. **Regular Inspections**

Check for:

- New cracks or holes
- Water stains
- Nail pops
- Corner bead damage

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I paint directly over spackling?

No. Spackling absorbs paint differently than drywall, causing "flashing." Always prime first.

### How many coats of joint compound do I need?

Minimum 3 coats for invisible repairs:

- First coat: Fill and tape
- Second coat: Build up and feather
- Third coat: Final smoothing

Large repairs may need 4-5 coats.

### Why do my repairs keep cracking?

**Possible causes:**

- Not letting compound dry fully
- House settling (structural issue)
- Wrong type of compound
- Application too thick

### Can I repair drywall myself or should I hire a pro?

**DIY if:**

- Small holes (under 2")
- You have 2-3 days
- Not water damaged
- Wall repair (not ceiling)

**Hire professional if:**

- Large damage
- Multiple repairs
- Water damage
- Ceiling work
- Texture matching needed
- Need it done fast

### How long does drywall repair take to dry?

- Small holes: 1-2 hours per coat
- Medium/large: 4-24 hours per coat
- Full repair: 2-5 days minimum
- Humid conditions: Add 50% more time

### How much does professional drywall repair cost?

- Full room: $500-$2,500
